# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ — English)

## What is Xafsizyol?
Xafsizyol (means "Safe Road" in Uzbek) is a free civic platform for reporting road potholes and hazards in Uzbekistan. It works as a Telegram Mini App — opens directly inside Telegram, no separate app download needed. Citizens report problems → authorities review → road gets fixed.

## How do I use it?
1. In the Telegram bot, press "Muammoni xabar qilish" (Report a problem)
2. Inside the Mini App, press the "+" button
3. Take a photo of the pothole (required)
4. Confirm the exact location on the map (GPS auto-detects)
5. Choose severity: Small / Medium / Critical
6. Write a short description (optional)
7. Enter your phone number (optional)
8. Press "Yuborish" (Submit)

## Is a photo required?
Yes. A photo is required to verify the reported issue. You cannot submit a report without a photo.

## Why enter a phone number?
Phone number is optional. It is only needed if road authorities need to contact you for additional details about the problem.

## When will my report be reviewed?
After submission the report gets "Pending" status. Authorities change it to "In Progress" when they start working on it, and "Fixed" when the road is repaired. You receive a Telegram notification for each status change.

## What do the statuses mean?
- **Pending (Kutilmoqda)** — new report, not yet reviewed by authorities
- **In Progress (Ko'rib chiqilmoqda)** — authorities have started working on the problem
- **Fixed (Bajarildi)** — problem resolved, road repaired

## Why should I vote on reports?
Voting means "I have the same problem here too." More votes = higher repair priority for authorities. If you see a pothole that affects you, vote for it to help get it fixed faster.

## Is the app free?
Yes, completely free for all citizens of Uzbekistan.

## My report is not showing on the map, what should I do?
Refresh the page. If still not visible, make sure you accurately confirmed the GPS location on the map during submission.

## Where can I see my reports?
Tap the profile icon in the bottom navigation — this opens the "My Reports" page with all your submitted reports.

## Who is this app for?
For any citizen of Uzbekistan who notices road hazards — drivers, pedestrians, cyclists. No registration needed — works through your Telegram account automatically.

## Which regions does it cover?
All of Uzbekistan — Tashkent city and all provinces: Tashkent region, Samarkand, Bukhara, Namangan, Fergana, Andijan, Kashkadarya, Surkhandarya, Khorezm, Navoi, Jizzakh, Syrdarya, Republic of Karakalpakstan.

## How should I describe the problem?
Keep it short and clear: "Large pothole in right lane", "Crack across full lane width", "Pothole fills with water after rain". Photo and location are the most important, but descriptions help authorities understand urgency.

## Can I report the same pothole someone else already reported?
Instead of creating a duplicate report, use the vote button on the existing report. This shows authorities that multiple people are affected by the same problem, which increases its priority.

## What happens after my report is marked Fixed?
The report stays visible on the map but is marked as Fixed (green status). You receive a Telegram notification. The report is filtered under the "Fixed" category.
