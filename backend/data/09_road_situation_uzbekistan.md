# Road Situation in Uzbekistan

## Overview

Road infrastructure in Uzbekistan has significant challenges, particularly in high-traffic urban areas and rural regions. Xafsizyol was built specifically to address this problem through citizen reporting.

## Common Problem Locations in Tashkent

High-traffic roads with frequent potholes:
- Amir Temur shoh ko'chasi (Amir Temur Avenue) — main city artery
- Chilonzor ko'chalari (Chilonzor district roads)
- Yunusabad district roads
- Mirzo Ulug'bek district roads
- Yakkasaroy district
- Sergeli district roads
- Shota Rustaveli ko'chasi
- Buyuk Ipak Yo'li (Great Silk Road avenue)

## Causes of Road Damage

- **Freeze-thaw cycles** — winter freezing and spring thaw cause asphalt to crack and heave
- **Heavy truck traffic** — overloaded trucks destroy road surfaces faster than designed
- **Poor drainage** — water accumulation weakens the road base
- **Old asphalt** — many roads were built during Soviet era and are past their service life
- **Summer heat** — extreme heat causes asphalt to soften and deform
- **Lack of timely maintenance** — small cracks that are not repaired quickly become large potholes

## Seasonal Patterns

- **Spring (March-May)** — worst season. Freeze-thaw cycle causes major cracking and pothole formation
- **Summer (June-August)** — heat expands existing cracks; repair season for authorities
- **Autumn (September-November)** — relatively stable but rain fills potholes with water making them harder to see
- **Winter (December-February)** — ice and snow hide potholes, making them especially dangerous

## Economic Impact

- Potholes cost Uzbek drivers millions of soums annually in vehicle damage:
  - Tire punctures and blowouts
  - Wheel rim damage
  - Suspension damage (shock absorbers, springs)
  - Wheel alignment issues
  - Undercarriage damage
- Commercial vehicle operators face higher costs due to more frequent driving
- Road accidents caused by potholes result in injury and property damage costs

## Responsible Authorities

- **Toshkent shahar yo'l xo'jaligi boshqarmasi** (Tashkent City Road Management Department) — responsible for Tashkent city roads
- **Regional Hokimiyat** (provincial governor's offices) — responsible for regional roads
- **O'zbekiston temir yo'llari** — railway crossing roads
- **Mahalla committees** — sometimes handle very local street repairs

## How Xafsizyol Helps

- Creates a centralized, geo-referenced database of all known road problems
- Vote counts show which problems affect the most people
- Photo evidence prevents false reports
- Status tracking ensures accountability
- Notification system closes the feedback loop between citizens and authorities
- Data can be used to prioritize repair budgets by severity and frequency
- Helps identify roads that need complete resurfacing vs. spot repairs

## Reporting Tips for Uzbekistan Conditions

- After heavy rain is a good time to spot new potholes — water reveals road damage
- Spring (March-April) is when most new potholes appear — report early
- Night or wet road potholes are especially dangerous — mark these as Critical
- Deep potholes on high-speed roads (60+ km/h) should always be marked Critical
- Report potholes at bus stops and pedestrian crossings — pedestrians are also at risk
